How Birth Control Work

Hormonal birth control uses synthetic hormones to regulate the body's natural processes, preventing ovulation, thickening cervical mucus, and altering the uterine lining to avoid pregnancy.

No Egg Release

Hormones in birth control prevent ovaries from releasing eggs, so fertilization can't occur.

Thick Mucus

Birth control makes cervical mucus thicker, blocking sperm from reaching eggs.

Thin Lining

Birth Control thins the uterine lining, making it hard for a fertilized egg to implant and grow.
